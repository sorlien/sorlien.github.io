Pancakes

A pancake tribute

The pancake, also known as hotcake, griddlecake or flapjack, is a (often) thin and round flat cake cooked on a hot surface. It is prepared from a starch-based batter that may contain eggs, milk and butter and usually fried with oil or butter. The pancake's shape and structure varies worldwide. In Britain for example, pancakes are often unleavened while in North America, a leavening agent such as baking powder is used. Pancakes may be served at any time of the day with a variety of toppings or fillings. And it's awesomely delicious!

Fun pancake facts

  1. The first ready-mix food which was sold commercially was Aunt Jemima pancake flour. It was invented in 1889 in St. Joseph, Missouri.

  2. World's largest pancake was cooked in Rochdale (Greater Manchester) in 1994, which was 15 metres in diameter, weighted three tonnes and had an estimated two million calories.

  3. In the U.S., Southerners eat the most pancakes, accounting for 32.5% of the pancake consumption.

  4. The world's most expensive pancake is available for $200.00 at Opus in the Printworks in England. Ingredients include Madagascan vanilla pods and 23-carat gold leaf.

  5. The town of Olney in England hosts an annual pancake race in celebration of "Pancake Day", also known as shrove tuesday. They've done it every year since 1445.

  6. In Sweden pancakes are called pannkakor. In France they are called crêpes and in Germany Pfannkuchen. Here you can learn more pancake names and read about the regional varieties.
