GELATO

WHAT IS GELATO?

Gelato typically has 50% less fat than traditional ice cream, but it tastes much creamier. The creamy texture comes from the way it’s made. Produced in small batches, slow churning instead of rapid beating. This produces a denser product served at -15 instead of -18, Low fat, rich creamy texture, intense flavour.

HOW TO IDENTIFY QUALITY GELATO

Appearance

Gelato should be a slightly milked-down, pastel version of its primary ingredient; it should almost never be bright and vibrant.

You should beware of gelaterias that stack their gelato into high piles. Since gelato oxidizes, having a lot of surface area exposed to the air is undesirable.

Gelato should also have a matte appearance instead of a shiny one. Shiny gelato is melting gelato. Over time, this gelato will melt and refreeze, damaging its structural integrity and texture.

Consistency
High quality gelato should have a spreadable consistency, unlike the much harder, more brittle ice cream.
Another consistency indicator to search for is how quickly it melts. True artisanal gelato should melt relatively quickly once out of the case; if it doesn’t, it was likely been made with too many emulsifiers and thickeners.

FUN FACTS ABAOUT GELATO

  1. The name of this frozen treat came from the word congelato which means "frozen."
  2. Eearly italian gelato was closer to sorbet than ice cream
  3. In 1686, Francesco Procopio dei Coltelli, a Sicilian fisherman and chef from Palermo, made the first gelato machine. He opened a café in Paris, the first gelato café in the world.
  4. Every other year, international teams compete at the Gelato World Cup (called Coppa del Mondo della Gelateria in Italian. Gelato world cup is actually a thing!
  5. Between 2009 and 2014, gelato sales increased dramatically from $11 million to $214 million. More and more Americans are buying gelato, and traditional ice cream companies have jumped on the gelato train.
  6. Gelato is similar to ice cream because it contains sugar, milk, and flavorings. The difference? Gelato has less cream and no egg yolks.
  7. The person selling and filling the gelato cups is called a gelatai.
  8. While ice cream is served with a rounded scoop, gelato shops generally use paddles. This technique adds to the traditional charm of this dessert.
  9. When you remove the milk from gelato and use water instead, you end up with a sorbetto which is a southern product.
  10. When discussing whether certain flavors go together, Italians use the phrase “marries well” – as in, “do these flavors marry well?” In Italian, that’s “questi gusti si sposano bene?”
